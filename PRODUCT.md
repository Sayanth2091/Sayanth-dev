# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Security engineers, threat hunters, CISOs, and hiring managers who review portfolios as a professional exercise — not passive visitors. They arrive skeptical, evaluate quickly, and remember texture. Occasionally: conference curators and award reviewers assessing craft.

## Product Purpose

NULL_SECTOR is a cinematic portfolio for Sayanth Sreekanth, SOC analyst and detection engineer. Six documented operations, each a case file. The scroll journey is the argument: a visitor who completes it understands both the person and the practitioner. Success is a hiring manager forwarding it internally without prompting.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Cinematic. Operator-grade. Not hacker-movie: no glitch effects for their own sake, no neon excess, no aesthetic borrowed from fiction. The tone is third-person — "He finds the ghosts in your machines" — distant, confident, slightly eerie. The site should feel like reading a declassified document, not watching a VFX reel.

## Anti-references

- Cyber-cliché portfolios: cyan-on-black, glitch text, terminal effects that exist only for mood
- Generic developer portfolios: skills bars, GitHub heatmaps, "I'm passionate about..."
- SaaS hero sections: big number + small label + gradient accent
- Any design whose theme and palette could be guessed from the job title alone

## Design Principles

1. **Escape the category reflex.** "Security portfolio" must not mean "dark + cyan". The aesthetic earns its darkness through scene, not genre.
2. **Narrative over decoration.** Every element serves the scroll story. Ornament that doesn't advance the narrative gets cut.
3. **Operator precision.** Copy is tight, confident, third-person. The voice doesn't hedge or explain itself.
4. **Texture through restraint.** The shard, grain, and typefaces do heavy lifting. Everything else holds back.
5. **The site is evidence.** The craft of the interface is itself a statement about the craft of the work inside it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` with nuance — don't kill opacity transitions or typed cadence, just disable layout-property animations and grain. Native cursor on form fields and touch devices.
